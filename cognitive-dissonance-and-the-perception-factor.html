Cognitive Dissonance and the Perception Factor

Overview

Cognitive dissonance is the mental and emotional discomfort experienced when a person holds conflicting beliefs, values, or behaviors. Rather than resolve the contradiction by aligning with truth, most people try to justify the inconsistency to protect their identity, comfort, or belonging.

In the Perception Factor (PF) framework, cognitive dissonance is a primary source of Excess Noise, causing the gap between perception and reality to widen. This distortion leads to lower PF, emotional defensiveness, and impaired decision-making.
