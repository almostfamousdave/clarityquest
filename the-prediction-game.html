The Prediction Game

A Perception Factor Group Challenge

🧠 What Is the Prediction Game?

The Prediction Game is a group-based activity designed to gamify growth in Perception Factor (PF), your ability to align your perception with reality. By making predictions, testing them against outcomes, and refining your understanding, you can reduce distortion, build epistemic humility, and improve clarity across life’s domains.

This game is safe, inclusive, and designed for families, friend groups, or teams who want to grow together in wisdom, not just knowledge.

🌱 Core Principles

  • PF can’t be forced. But it can be invited through voluntary, thoughtful play.
  • Predictions reveal perception. By comparing expectations to outcomes, we expose distortion or clarity.
  • Growth happens through feedback. Scoring and review help participants calibrate, not compete.
  • Humility is rewarded. Admitting wrong views earns points, not shame.

👥 Who Can Play?

  • Families
  • Study groups
  • Friends or youth groups
  • Leadership or ministry teams

All that’s required is trust, curiosity, and a shared desire to grow in clarity.

🧩 How to Play

1. Set a Timeframe

  • Daily, weekly, or monthly rounds; choose your pace

2. Make Predictions

Each player writes down 1–3 thoughtful, testable predictions for the upcoming round. The goal is not to guess, but to reveal and test how clearly you see.

Use the Prediction Form below to structure your entry.

📝 Prediction Form

Use this form to submit thoughtful, clarity-building predictions during each round of the Prediction Game.

🔮 Prediction:

What do you think will happen?
Be specific and avoid vague, trivial, or guaranteed outcomes.

Example: “I predict that Henry will push back against the new policy in tomorrow’s meeting.”

⏳ Timeframe (Deadline):

When will we know if you were right?
Tie it to a date, event, or specific window.

Example: “By end of the meeting on Tuesday,” or “Before Sunday at 10pm.”

🎯 Reasoning (Why do you think this?):

What insight, pattern, or judgment led you to this prediction?
Avoid simply repeating external data (like weather forecasts or headlines) unless you’re adding original interpretation.

Good: “The weather app shows sunshine, but I noticed thick cloud movement this morning.”
Good: “This person tends to reject rules that challenge their autonomy.”

📉 What Would Prove This Wrong?

What outcome would clearly falsify your prediction?
This helps test the clarity and sharpness of your lens.

Example: “If Henry supports the policy or doesn’t respond at all, I’ll consider this prediction wrong.”

📊 Confidence Level (0–10):

How sure are you, really?
Be honest. Overconfidence can reveal distortion; underconfidence can reveal avoidant behavior.

0 = pure guess
10 = absolute certainty (should be rare!)

🏛️ Optional: PF Domain(s) Touched (if any):

Use this to reflect or discuss, not score.

  • Emotional
  • Relational
  • Moral
  • Political
  • Spiritual
  • Cultural
  • Intellectual
  • Historical
  • Empirical
  • Self-Perception

Example: “This touches the Emotional and Relational domains—I’m interpreting a pattern in someone’s behavior.”

✅ Checklist for a Strong Prediction
  • Is it uncertain? Could I be wrong?
  • Is it testable? Could others clearly verify the outcome?
  • Is it time-bound?
  • Does it reveal something about how I perceive reality—not just what I’ve read?
  • Am I adding clarity, not just parroting data?

“Clarity isn’t about guessing right. It’s about seeing cleanly—and adjusting when you don’t.”

3. Opposition (Optional)

Other players may “oppose” your prediction if they believe you’ll be wrong. This makes things interesting, but isn’t required.

4. Outcome Review

After the timeframe passes, revisit each prediction:

  • Was it accurate?
  • What distorted or clarified perception?
  • Did anyone update their view?

🧮 Scoring System

SituationPoints
Correct prediction (solo)+1
Correct prediction w/ opposition+1 +1 per opponent
Incorrect prediction-1 (optional)
Publicly admit a mistaken prediction+1 bonus
Correctly oppose someone+1
Changed your mind mid-game (before outcome)+0.5

🎖 Bonus categories (optional):

  • Most honest moment
  • Best self-correction
  • Strongest steelmanned view

🎯 Prediction Accuracy vs Reasoning Accuracy

Sometimes a prediction is right, but for the wrong reasons. In PF terms, this is not true clarity; it’s coincidence or lucky distortion.

Outcome ≠ Clear Perception

🎲 Scoring Logic

OutcomeReasoningPoints
✅ Correct & clear✅ Sound reasoningFull points (e.g., +1 or +3)
✅ Correct❌ Faulty reasoningHalf points (e.g., +0.5 or +1)
❌ Wrong❌ Faulty reasoning0 or -1
❌ Wrong✅ Sound reasoning, surprising miss+0.5 (optional – reward process)

🔍 How to Judge Reasoning

During Outcome Review, ask:

  • Was the reasoning testable or just a guess?
  • Would this logic predict the right outcome in other scenarios?
  • Did their logic contradict what happened?

Let the group or the Clarity Coach help guide this gently and fairly.

📝 Example

Prediction: “I predict the power bill will be under $200 because it’ll be cooler next week.”
Outcome: It was under $200, but the AC broke, not because it got cooler.

🧠 Reasoning was wrong.
🏆 Award: +0.5 (partial credit for outcome, but distorted perception)

“Don’t reward luck as if it’s clarity. That’s how distortion gets reinforced.”

🛡️ Inclusion Without Pressure

🤝 What About Non-Participants?

Not everyone may want to play. That’s okay, and expected. PF change must be chosen, not forced.

Alternative roles to stay connected:

  • Observer/Scribe – tracks scores, patterns, or group predictions
  • Reflection Prompter – asks clarifying or PF-deepening questions
  • Weekly Insight Sharer – contributes a thought, quote, or journal reflection

“You don’t have to play right now. Just being here is part of the process.”

🧩 Optional Role: PF Mentor

To support younger or less-developed players, the Prediction Game can include a PF Mentor role. The goal is not to win, but to grow together in clarity.

👤 What Is a PF Mentor?

A PF Mentor is a more experienced player who partners with a newer or struggling participant to:

  • Encourage thoughtful predictions
  • Guide reasoning without controlling the outcome
  • Promote clarity over correctness
  • Normalize mistakes as part of growth

A PF Mentor helps the player polish their own lens, not replace it with someone else’s.

🛠️ How It Works

1. Pairing Up

  • A mentor may volunteer to work with a player for a single round or multiple rounds.
  • Mentors and mentees can rotate over time to avoid dependency.

2. Co-Prediction (Optional)

  • The mentee may submit a Co-Prediction with their mentor.
  • The mentor may assist with clarity and framing but the mentee must write the final prediction.
  • Co-predictions are scored only for the mentee.

Format the label like this:

🔁 Co-Prediction with [Mentor Name]

3. Mentor Feedback Bonus (Optional)

  • If a mentee shares how their mentor helped them gain clarity, the mentor may earn a +0.5 bonus point.
  • This only applies if the mentee voluntarily reflects on the value of the interaction.

This reinforces humility and service, not score-hoarding.

4. Mentee Reflection Prompts (Optional)

Add to the bottom of the Prediction Form:

  • Did someone help you see more clearly this round? If so, how?
  • What question or insight shifted your thinking most?

These questions deepen awareness and invite honest gratitude.

❗ Safeguard: No Score Sharing

Mentors do not earn or lose points based on the mentee’s predictions.
Only the mentee receives the standard scoring.
Mentor bonuses apply only for reflection-based clarity support.

Scoring integrity is maintained. Growth remains personal.

✅ Why This Is Worth Adding

  • Supports low-scoring or younger players without isolating them
  • Models epistemic humility and patience
  • Keeps the game safe, inclusive, and growth-centered
  • Reinforces the truth that clarity is learned together

Mentoring is not coaching a win, it’s companioning a lens toward truth.

🧭 Keeping Predictions Meaningful

🔒 Rule of Relevance

Predictions must involve:

  • A real-world event
  • Happening within a set timeframe
  • That others can verify

❌ Trivial: “The sun will rise tomorrow.”
✅ Relevant: “I predict I’ll be awake before sunrise and take a photo of it.”

🧠 Rule of Uncertainty

A valid prediction must involve real ambiguity or a chance of being wrong. In other words, it should not be a guaranteed outcome or a self-fulfilling act.

Predictions like “the sun will rise tomorrow” or “I will eat dinner tonight” don’t help you grow in perception, they’re either universally certain or entirely under your control.

The goal is to test your lens on things you can’t control, but can observe.

✅ Examples of good uncertainty:

  • “The stock market will dip below 34,000 this week.”
  • “Emma will call to cancel her plans before Friday.”
  • “I will wake up before 6:00 a.m. tomorrow without an alarm.”

❌ Examples of invalid certainty:

  • “The sun will rise tomorrow.”
  • “I will take a breath today.”
  • “Someone will speak during the meeting.”

Ask yourself: Could I be wrong about this?
If the answer is no, the prediction likely fails the Rule of Uncertainty.

🧪 Rule of Testability

Every prediction must be falsifiable, which means it must be possible to prove it wrong if it doesn’t happen.

A prediction like “I sense bad energy in the air” or “People will eventually wake up to the truth” cannot be tested. If the outcome is vague or unmeasurable, there’s no way to compare it to reality.

Without testability, you’re not improving clarity, you’re just expressing a belief.

✅ Testable predictions:

  • “It will rain here by Saturday.”
  • “The team will miss their deadline this week.”
  • “I will receive at least one unsolicited compliment today.”

❌ Untestable predictions:

  • “Something weird will happen eventually.”
  • “I’ll feel better after making this prediction.”
  • “The government is hiding something.”

Ask yourself: Would others clearly know if I was wrong?
If not, it’s not testable.

🗓️ Rule of Time-Bound Clarity

A prediction must include a clear and specific timeframe. Without a deadline, you can’t determine when to evaluate it, or even if it came true at all.

Vague predictions like “I will get in shape” or “He’ll regret that decision someday” stretch into the future with no endpoint. They become impossible to verify.

Tying your prediction to a date, time, or concrete event turns it into a growth opportunity.

✅ Time-bound examples:

  • “By next Monday, I predict the repair won’t be done.”
  • “Within the next 3 days, I predict she’ll change her mind.”
  • “I predict I’ll forget to bring the documents to tomorrow’s meeting.”

❌ Timeless predictions:

  • “Eventually, this will all make sense.”
  • “At some point, they’ll get caught.”
  • “He’ll apologize someday.”

Ask yourself: When will we check to see if I was right?
If you can’t answer that easily, add a time frame.

🔎 Depth Matters: Avoid Surface-Only Predictions

Predictions that simply repeat obvious data (like weather apps or public headlines) may not qualify unless they include original insight or interpretation.

To grow in PF, aim for:

  • Personal synthesis of information
  • Predictions that expose your own bias or assumptions
  • Human behavior, motivation, and nuance

“You don’t get points for reading radar. You get points for seeing clearly where others miss it.”

The group may ask: “Is this prediction just repeating known data, or is it testing your lens?”

💡 Optional Tier System

TierDescriptionMax Points
EasyCommon or expected+1
MediumRequires insight or self-awareness+2
High RiskCounterintuitive or unpopular+3 (if right), -1 (if wrong)

Let the group vote on tier before locking it in.

🧙‍♂️ Who Decides If a Prediction Is Too Vague?

The group does, together.

👥 Group Check Format:

“Is this prediction clear, time-bound, and meaningful enough to test?”

If even one person objects, the predictor can:

  • Refine the prediction, or
  • Choose a different one

👑 Optional Role: Clarity Coach

Assign one person per round to serve as a gentle referee:

  • Helps clarify fuzzy predictions
  • Encourages refinement without judgment

“That one’s a little too broad, can you add a timeframe or define what success looks like?”

🔄 Integration With PF Training

Use this game as part of your Perception Factor lifestyle:

  • Truth Journal Prompt: Did my prediction match reality? Why or why not?
  • Noise Scan: What emotions, media, or assumptions influenced me?
  • Discomfort Scan: Was I avoiding a painful truth?

Link to PF Core Practices like the Question Loop, Steelmanning, and Belief-Outcome Journaling.

🏆 Why It Works

Because it rewards the behaviors that raise PF:

BehaviorPF Effect
Making clear, testable predictionsReveals cognitive clarity or distortion
Welcoming feedbackBuilds humility, lowers noise
Updating beliefs over timeReduces bias, increases flexibility
Failing safelyRemoves fear of growth
Admitting you were wrongStrengthens alignment with truth

“Clarity is confrontational to distortion. This game lets that confrontation be gentle and shared.”

📜 Closing Thought

“You don’t rise in PF by being smarter.
You rise by refusing to lie to yourself, and learning to see through noise.”

Want to play? Start with one prediction. The truth will meet you there.
